JAH PRAYZAH

Zimbabwe's King of Afro Pop

Mukudzeyi Mukombe, known professionally as Jah Prayzah, is Zimbabwe's most celebrated contemporary musician. Known as "Musoja" (The Soldier), he has revolutionized Zimbabwean music by blending traditional sounds with modern Afro-pop, creating a unique style that has captured hearts across Africa and beyond.

With multiple awards including several Zimbabwe Music Awards and recognition across Africa, Jah Prayzah continues to put Zimbabwe on the global music map through his innovative sound and captivating performances.

Travel Destinations

Top Zimbabwe Destinations

Victoria Falls

One of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World, Victoria Falls is a spectacular waterfall on the Zambezi River at the border between Zambia and Zimbabwe.

Activities: Bungee jumping, white water rafting, helicopter tours, sunset cruises

Hwange National Park

Zimbabwe's largest national park, home to one of the largest elephant populations in Africa and all of the Big Five animals.

Activities: Game drives, walking safaris, bird watching, photography

Great Zimbabwe Ruins

Ancient city ruins that gave Zimbabwe its name. UNESCO World Heritage Site showcasing the country's rich historical heritage.

Activities: Guided tours, cultural experiences, museum visits, photography

Matobo Hills

Unique granite rock formations creating a mystical landscape. Home to ancient rock art and Cecil Rhodes' grave.

Activities: Rock climbing, cave paintings tours, rhino tracking, cultural sites

Eastern Highlands

Mountain ranges along the Mozambique border offering cool climate, waterfalls, and scenic hiking trails.

Activities: Hiking, trout fishing, tea plantation tours, waterfall visits

Lake Kariba

One of the world's largest man-made lakes, perfect for fishing, boat cruises, and watching spectacular African sunsets.

Activities: Fishing, boat cruises, houseboat stays, wildlife viewing

Discover Zimbabwe

About Zimbabwe

Zimbabwe is a landlocked country in southern Africa, known for its dramatic landscape and diverse wildlife. Home to Victoria Falls, one of the world's largest waterfalls, and ancient ruins that tell the story of a great civilization.

Capital: Harare | Population: 15+ million | Languages: English, Shona, Ndebele

Wildlife & Nature

Experience the Big Five in Hwange National Park, witness the annual elephant migration, and discover over 350 bird species. Zimbabwe's diverse ecosystems range from tropical forests to granite hills.

Best Time: May-October | Parks: 5 National Parks | Species: 350+ birds, Big Five

Rich Culture

Discover the legacy of Great Zimbabwe, explore traditional villages, and experience the warmth of Zimbabwean hospitality. From stone sculptures to vibrant music, culture is alive everywhere.

Heritage Sites: 5 UNESCO Sites | Arts: Stone sculpture, music | Festivals: Year-round
